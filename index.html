Soil Reference
SoilDrainageNutrientsBest ForAvoid ForNotes
FoxFarm Ocean Forest Moderate High (NPK enriched, compost, worm castings) Most tropicals, begonias, philodendrons, pothos, ficus, caladiums, nerve plants Carnivorous plants, orchids, succulents/cacti, fresh cuttings Rich out of the bag — can be too hot for seedlings or sensitive roots. Add perlite (20–30%) for better drainage. Most of your Naked Root plants are in this.
FoxFarm + Perlite Good High Hoyas, begonias, trailing plants that want nutrients but need to dry faster Carnivorous, orchids Standard upgrade for plants prone to root rot. ~70/30 mix. Your Hoya compacta, Polka Dot Begonia, and Rope Plant use this.
Rosy Aroid Mix Excellent Low–Medium Monsteras, anthuriums, philodendrons, pothos — any aroid Plants that need moisture retention (ferns, nerve plants) Chunky bark-based mix with excellent aeration. Roots stay healthy, less compaction over time. Your Flaming Lily and recommended for Monstera adansonii.
Orchid Mix (bark-based) Excellent None Phalaenopsis orchids, some hoyas Everything else — drains too fast for most plants Primarily bark chunks. Dries fast. Fertilize regularly since there's no nutritional base. Your Moth Orchid is in this.
Sphagnum Moss Low (moisture-retentive) None Jewel orchids, carnivorous plants, high-humidity terrariums, cuttings Succulents, cacti, most tropicals long-term Holds water extremely well. Can compact and become anaerobic over time — refresh annually. Your Jewel Orchid (Naked Root) uses this.
Peat + Perlite 50/50 Moderate None (intentionally) Carnivorous plants ONLY (Venus Fly Traps, Sundews, Pitchers) Everything else — nutrient-free by design Must use distilled or rainwater. Nutrients kill carnivorous plants. Never substitute with regular potting mix.
Cactus / Succulent Mix Excellent Low Cacti, succulents, Crown of Thorns, String of Hearts, String of Dolphins, Albuca, Zwartkop Tropicals, moisture-loving plants Fast-draining by design. Can add extra coarse sand or perlite for even faster drainage. Most of your Logee's succulents should go into this.
Bonsai Mix (Akadama/Pumice) Excellent None Bonsai trees (Juniper, Serissa) Everything except bonsai Inorganic, granular. Designed for bonsai root structure and regular fertilizing. Don't substitute with regular potting mix for bonsai.
Bromeliad / Orchid Bark Mix Excellent Low Guzmania and other bromeliads, epiphytes Moisture-loving plants Bromeliads don't use soil for nutrients — they feed through their central cup. Soil is just an anchor. Keep cup filled with clean water.
Fertilizer Reference
Fertilizer TypeNPK ProfileBest ForWhenNever Use OnNotes
Balanced Liquid (e.g. 20-20-20) Equal N-P-K General tropicals, foliage plants, philodendrons, pothos, dracaenas, palms Every 2–4 weeks during growing season (spring–summer). Monthly or none in winter. Carnivorous plants, newly repotted plants, dormant plants Dilute to half-strength or less. Your False Shamrock was fertilized 2026-03-31 — likely with something in this category.
High-Nitrogen (e.g. 3-1-2) N-heavy Foliage-focused plants — philodendrons, pothos, ferns, palms, nerve plants Active growing season only Flowering plants (inhibits blooms), carnivorous plants, dormant plants Promotes lush green leaves. Good for the Parlor Palm, Ginseng Ficus, Pink Princess variegation growth.
Bloom / High-Phosphorus (e.g. 10-30-20) P-heavy Orchids, anthuriums, begonias, bromeliads (when pushing blooms), Crown of Thorns When buds are forming or you want to trigger flowering Purely foliage plants, carnivorous plants Switch back to balanced after blooming. Good for your Moth Orchid and Flaming Lily.
Orchid Fertilizer (e.g. 30-10-10 or balanced urea-free) Varies — urea-free important Moth Orchid, Jewel Orchid, Lightning Jewel Orchid Every watering at quarter-strength ("weakly, weekly") during active growth Carnivorous plants, succulents, cacti Must be urea-free — orchid roots can't process urea. Dilute heavily.
Cactus / Succulent Fertilizer (low nitrogen) Low N, higher P-K (e.g. 2-7-7) Cacti, succulents, Crown of Thorns, Albuca, Zwartkop, Fairy Castle Cactus Once in spring, once in summer. Never in dormancy. Tropical foliage plants, carnivorous plants Less is more. Over-fertilizing succulents causes etiolation and weak growth.
Bonsai Fertilizer Balanced with micronutrients (e.g. 10-10-10) Juniper Bonsai, Serissa Bonsai Every 2 weeks spring–summer, monthly in fall, none in winter dormancy Carnivorous plants, plants in plain water Bonsai soil has no nutrients — regular fertilizing is essential, not optional.
Never Fertilize — Venus Fly Traps, Cape Sundew, Trumpet Pitcher — any carnivorous plant. They get nutrients from prey. Fertilizer kills them.
